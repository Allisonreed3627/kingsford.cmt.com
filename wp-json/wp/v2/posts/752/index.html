A firefighting helicopter crashed in Cabazon on Sunday evening after a midair collision with another helicopter fighting the same fire, killing all three crew members aboard, authorities said. The other helicopter managed to land safely.<\/p>\n

Those killed aboard what was described as an observer Bell helicopter were a Cal Fire division chief, a Cal Fire captain and a contract pilot, said Cal Fire Southern Region Chief David Fulcher. He and other fire personnel wore black tape over their badges during a midnight news conference as they mourned the department\u2019s loss.<\/p>\n

A Sikorsky S-64 helicopter, better known as a \u201cSkycrane,\u201d with two aboard was able to land safely following the collision, Fulcher said.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhile engaged in the firefight, two helicopters collided,\u201d he said. \u201cTragically, all three members perished\u201d who were aboard the helicopter that crashed.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis was a tragic loss for the community, the fire service community at Cal Fire and the Riverside County Fire Department,\u201d Fulcher said.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhile this was a tragic event, we are thankful it wasn\u2019t worse,\u201d he said. \u201cThe individuals in the first helicopter were able to land safely without incident and no one else was hurt.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe think about this and how it affects the families. We have lost three great individuals. Three fathers. Three husbands. Three friends. Three sons,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

After the crash, emergency personnel were called to the area near Apache Trail and Bonita Avenue\/Pipeline Road around 7:20 p.m. for an air emergency, according to the Riverside County Sheriff\u2019s Department. Cabazon is a community along the 10 Freeway east of Banning. The crash happened south of the 10.<\/p>\n
