EL CAJON, Calif. \u2014 Just one day after San Diego city officials approved the use of 500 Smart Streetlights<\/a>, the City of\u00a0El\u00a0Cajon\u00a0is gearing up to start using its new cameras to track license plates.<\/p>\n

Forty new sets of eyes \u2014 by way of surveillance cameras \u2014 will soon call the streets of\u00a0El\u00a0Cajon\u00a0home. The cameras will track license plates in real time, an effort police say to get criminals off the streets quicker.<\/p>\n

\u201cTime is the biggest impact on our job, time to investigate crimes and having this real-time information to respond immediately to get people in custody or recover stolen property,\u201d said Lt. Jeremiah Larson with the El Cajon Police Department.<\/p>\n

Police activated the cameras for a brief test run last week and say in that short time, the cameras tracked two cars that were reported in violent crimes \u2014 one was involved in a carjacking and robbery and another was a stolen vehicle.<\/p>\n

Police say thanks to the cameras, the suspects were arrested.<\/p>\n

<\/aside>\n

\u201cWe take public safety very seriously in\u00a0El\u00a0Cajon. We have a fully staffed police department and we are doing everything we can to keep things as safe as possible, so that means we\u2019re are going to do everything at our disposal to keep criminals off our streets,\u201d El Cajon Mayor Bill Wells said.<\/p>\n

\n<\/aside>\n

For some people, the new technology feels like an invasion of privacy. Some residents worry the tracking goes too far.<\/p>\n

Others, however, say anything that helps keep police keep criminals off the streets, the better.<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t mind it, they\u2019re already tracking us on our cell phone, just an added layer of security, don\u2019t think it\u2019ll impact us in\u00a0El\u00a0Cajon,\u201d Charlie Galaviz said.<\/p>\n

\u201cI think that\u2019s awesome, less people I have to worry about, the better for my family\u2026the hard working people of\u00a0El\u00a0Cajon,\u201d Patricia Joyce said.<\/p>\n

\n<\/aside>\n

Police say the cameras will not track anything but license plates, and that information is deleted after 30 days unless it\u2019s connected to a car involved in a crime.<\/p>\n

The cameras are expected to be up and officially running by the end of the month.<\/p>\n
